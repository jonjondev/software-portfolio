More About Me

Based in Sydney, Australia, I am a recent graduate of an honours degree by research at UTS, having written my thesis on interactive drama systems in video games. I was particularly interested in building games that lean into emergent properties to provide players with dramatically interesting gameplay. As a result, my work was published at the IEEE Conference on Games (CoG) 2020 for a paper titled "A Review of Agency Architectures in Interactive Drama Systems".

Previously I had graduated from the University of Technology Sydney, with a Distinction in BSc. Information Technology, majoring in enterprise systems development and particularly focusing on web/mobile apps development as well as interaction design. Throughout the course I was afforded the opportunity to build up a broad skillset in various programming languages, frameworks and techniques in software engineering.

In early 2019 I was also invited to present a game narrative review paper I had written at GDC, winning gold for my critical analysis of the game Aviary Attorney as selected by a panel of industry veteran narrative designers. And in 2020 I was named as one of the Australian Financial Review's (AFR) Top 100 Future Leaders, a competition that identifies the most promising graduates across multiple disciplines each year.

I love programming and enjoy spending my free time learning new tools and languages, which has provided me with a great deal of flexibility with regard to the kinds of projects I am able to work on, from games, to networking tools, and even analytics. However I also enjoy (and have experience with) a broad range of creative fields, including: gameplay and narrative design, UX&I design, music production and composition, and video editing.

If you were to ask me about what gets me out of bed in the morning, I would say it's making cool stuff. At my core, I am a creator, and I love to craft experiences, software, games, and many more amazing things. I've developed a passion for learning in service of creation, so if I'm not working hard on one of my projects, I'm likely at a game jam, learning a new skill, or off hiking somewhere.

Other Things I've Done

A Review of Agency Architectures in Interactive Drama Systems

In August of 2020, I had my games design research paper on AI and narrative design titled "A Review of Agency Architectures in Interactive Drama Systems" published in the proceedings of the IEEE Conference on Games (CoG) 2020. The paper is a review of the interactive drama field in video games research, re-framing the field's key literature as existing upon a spectrum based on their use of scripted or emergent properties - theorising and proposing a new style of hybrid, emergent-scripted solution to drama manager design.

During the conference, I delivered a recorded presentation, summarising the paper's content, findings, and providing additional background on the research area of the interactive drama field. The paper was eventually adopted as the base of my honours research into agent-based drama management systems.

GDC Game Narrative Review & Poster Presentation

In early 2019 I was invited to attend and present my work at GDC after winning gold for a paper I had written, as selected by a panel of industry veteran narrative designers.

The paper lays out my critiques of the narrative design of the game Aviary Attorney, applauding it for its use of setting to punctuate the themes of the game, as well as its broad-over-deep approach to the branching narrative. While some of the game's dialogue falls a bit flat, especially with regards to tone in certain sections, it is altogether a quality experience that deftly illustrates the powers unique to an interactive genre.

At the conference, I took part in the poster presentation session, where I was able to exhibit a poster that summarises my paper in a visual style to GDC attendees and a select number of professional game narrative designers.
